Where friction usually appears

The useful audit begins where the customer journey breaks.

A digital setup can look complete while still creating uncertainty for customers or unnecessary manual work for the business.

01

Discovery friction

Customers find the business but cannot quickly understand the service, location, or next step.

02

Decision friction

Information exists, but the customer does not know which option, service, or action fits their situation.

03

Contact friction

Forms, calls, and booking paths collect too little context or create unnecessary steps.

04

Follow-up friction

Customer activity is spread across disconnected tools, inboxes, calendars, or manual reminders.

What you may receive

Useful direction, carefully framed.

The audit inquiry is designed to clarify the discussion. It does not promise a formal certification, technical inspection or guaranteed provider match.

  1. Possible setup observations

    Plain-language notes about friction or unclear connections visible from the supplied context.

  2. Practical priority areas

    A small set of categories that may be worth exploring before broader changes.

  3. Questions to clarify

    Missing details about workflow, responsibility, customer consent or compatibility.

  4. Suitable service categories

    Relevant Nexlocal pathways based on the information submitted.

  5. Suggested next-step discussion

    A possible route for gathering more information or connecting with an independent specialist.
