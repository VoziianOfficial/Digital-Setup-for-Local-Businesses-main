Customer booking journey

Reduce uncertainty at each decision.

A booking journey is more than a calendar. It should explain what is being selected and set a realistic expectation for the next step.

  1. 01

    Choose the service

    Use clear names, duration context and suitability notes.

  2. 02

    Select availability

    Show times that reflect real capacity and operating rules.

  3. 03

    Provide essential details

    Collect only the information needed to prepare the appointment.

  4. 04

    Receive confirmation

    Explain the status, location and any relevant next action.

Availability and workflow

Define the rules the calendar cannot guess.

The useful booking setup depends on people, services, locations, preparation and internal responsibility.

Account for preparation, cleanup, travel or notes rather than exposing every calendar gap.

Decide when customers should choose a person or location and when internal assignment is more appropriate.

Clarify whether an appointment is confirmed immediately, pending review or subject to another step.

Make modification expectations visible and align the workflow with applicable business terms.

Collect proportionate information and explain how reminders or other communications are handled.
